# Wise Homeopathy Content Guidelines
## Version 2.0

> Defines how every word on the Wise Homeopathy website should be written.

# Content Philosophy

Write for patients, not clinicians.

Every page should:
- Build trust
- Reduce anxiety
- Answer questions quickly
- Encourage informed decisions

---

# Tone of Voice

- Calm
- Professional
- Compassionate
- Clear
- Evidence-aware
- Honest

Avoid:
- Marketing hype
- Fear-based language
- Medical jargon without explanation
- Unsupported claims

---

# Writing Rules

- One idea per paragraph.
- Maximum 3–4 lines per paragraph.
- Use headings every few sections.
- Prefer bullets, tables and timelines over long blocks of text.

---

# Page Structure

1. Short answer
2. Simple explanation
3. Detailed information
4. FAQ
5. Call to Action

---

# Headlines

Every page needs:
- One H1
- Logical H2/H3 hierarchy
- Descriptive headings

---

# Calls to Action

Use contextual CTAs such as:
- Book Consultation
- Start Online Consultation
- Meet Our Doctors
- Read Related Article

Avoid repetitive "Book Now" buttons.

---

# Trust Content

Distribute trust throughout the page:
- Qualifications
- Research
- Transparent consultation process
- Clinic images
- Patient education

---

# Medical Content

- Never promise cures.
- Clearly distinguish education from medical advice.
- Encourage consultation where appropriate.

---

# Readability

Target:
- Plain English
- Short sentences
- Active voice
- Scannable layout

---

# Content Review Checklist

- Is it useful?
- Is it clear?
- Is it accurate?
- Is it trustworthy?
- Does it guide the reader to the next step?
